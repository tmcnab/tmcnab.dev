# Ultralight

For as long as I've had cognisance of the world I've wanted to fly.

I studied the every subject in High School so that I could fly the [F-111 Aardvark]() for the [RAAF](). Alas, and crushingly as to me pursuing my aspirations, it was not meant to be: I had a genetic defect that gave me [Partial Red-Green Color Blindness](). As a gamer I've flown GA, regionals, widebodies and military aircraft for thousands of hours performing a wide range of activities. And yet, it's always left me wanting to do more. To really fly between airfields and airports - to experience the joy of flight.

A few years ago I learned that I could take one of three paths to flight:
1. Light Sport Aircraft (LSA), a 10 hour course
2. General Aviation (GA), a minimum of 40 hours learning; or
3. Part 103 Ultralights which require no training or certification

As an engineer my inner optimist wanted to min-max an ultralight with electric propulsion technology. In 2014 Airbus flew a dual-ducted-fan electric aircraft crossed the English Channel. Ten years later we should be able to create an ultralight aircraft using modern materials.
